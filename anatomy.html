Cornea


The cornea is the transparent, dome-shaped surface that covers the front of the eye.

Retina


The retina is a layer of tissue in the back of the eye that senses light and sends images to your brain.

Iris


The colored part of the eye

Pupil


Is the black circle at the center of the iris.

Sclera


The white part of the eye

Conjunctiva


The soft, thin, transparent loose mucus membrane that covers the sclera as well as the inside of the lids.

Limbus


This is the transition zone were the cornea and sclera meet.

Outermost Layer


Together the cornea, sclera, conjunctiva and limbus form the eye's outermost layer

Anterior Chamber


This is a fluid filled space between the cornea and iris.

Posterior Chamber


This is a fluid filled space between the iris and crystalline lens.

Crystalline Lens


A flexible, transparent structure that is located in the posterior chamber behind the iris and in front of the vitreous body.

Ciliary Muscles


These muscles control the shape of the crystalline lens.

Vitreous humor


is a gel like substance that fills the eye between the crystalline lens and retina.

Choroid


A layer of tissue in the eye that sits between the sclera and retina.

Retina


A light sensitive tissue at the back of the eye.

Retinal Detachment


This is when the retina detaches from the choroid, the tissue responsible for providing nourishment to it.

Macular Degeneration


An ocular condition that affects a small area located at the center of the retina that's responsible for sharp detailed vision call the macula.

Optic disc


A circular area located at the back of the eye where the optic nerve exits the retina.

Optic Nerve


The optical nerve is a bundle of nerve fibers that carries visual information from the retina to the brain.
